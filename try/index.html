Bayaning 3rd World

"Bayaning 3rd World" is a 2000 Filipino mockumentary film directed by Mike de Leon. The narrative follows two filmmakers, portrayed by Ricky Davao and Cris Villanueva,
who embark on an investigative journey to create a film about José Rizal, the Philippines' national hero. Their quest delves into the complexities surrounding Rizal's life, particularly the
debated issue of whether he retracted his anti-Catholic sentiments before his execution. Throughout their investigation, they interview key figures such as Rizal's mother Doña Teodora,
his siblings, and his common-law wife Josephine Bracken, uncovering conflicting testimonies and personal biases that challenge their understanding of heroism and historical narrative.


Critically acclaimed for its innovative approach, the film was included in the Manunuri ng Pelikulang Pilipino's list of the Ten Best Films of the Decade. Despite its critical success, it did not
achieve significant box office performance. The film's self-reflexive style and exploration of historical complexities offer a unique perspective on national identity and the construction of hero
narratives in post-colonial Philippines.
