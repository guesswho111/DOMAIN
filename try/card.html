José Rizal was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary who is widely regarded as the national hero of the Philippines. Born on June 19, 1861, Rizal was a polymath, excelling in various fields such as medicine, literature, and the arts. His novels Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo exposed the injustices and abuses of the Spanish colonial government and the Catholic Church in the Philippines. His writings inspired the Philippine revolution and ultimately led to his execution by the Spanish on December 30, 1896, making him a martyr for Philippine independence.
